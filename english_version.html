Kodo Ishibashi Shakuhachi Performer

Born in 2000 in Tokyo, Kodo began studying under Haruto Fujii after participating in a trial class held by the Setagaya Sankyoku Association at the age of 9.

After graduating from the Tokyo University of the Arts Music High School and the Department of Japanese Traditional Music (Shakuhachi), he is currently in his second year of the Master's program at the same university.

During his studies, he has studied under master shakuhachi players such as Reibo Aoki, Komei Takemura, Yosuke Kawase, Hozan Nomura, and Tsuneo Yoshida.

He has received awards such as the "Ataka Prize," "Ryohei Miyata Prize," "Dou Voice Society Prize," and "Acanthus Music Award" during his undergraduate years.

He performed at the "2022 Music University Graduates Concert" held at the Imperial Palace's Momokaku Hall.

In addition to classical works, he actively engages with pop music and contemporary Japanese music, expanding his repertoire.

Member of the Japanese Sankyoku Association, Koto-Ko-Ryu Association, Setagaya Sankyoku Association, and Dou Voice Society.
